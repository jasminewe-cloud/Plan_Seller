Task 4
If renting or keeping: what that involves from abroad
Both paths keep you in the home long-term. They have very different cost and complexity profiles.
Renting from abroad is manageable but requires setup before you leave. A property manager is essential. The carrying cost path is simple but expensive — make sure you're clear on the monthly number.

Property manager typically charges 8–10% of monthly rent. Costs above assume 3-bed at $3,250/mo market rate.
What managing from abroad involves
Finding and vetting a property manager before you leave (allow 3–4 weeks)
Setting up a local maintenance contact for emergencies
Understanding Paris time zone for communication (9 hours ahead of Seattle)
Keeping a reserve fund for unexpected repairs (recommend 3–6 months of costs)
If keeping vacant
Is there someone you trust to house-sit or check in regularly?
Vacant homes still need maintenance, pest control, and periodic walkthroughs
Vacancy may affect your homeowner's insurance — check your policy

  3. Do you have property management referrals for owners going abroad?
  4. What's your read on the current buyer pool for a home like mine?
  5. Is there any benefit to waiting vs. listing now given market conditions?
